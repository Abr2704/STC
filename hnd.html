Level 4 & 5 Computing (Cyber Security Pathway)

Our Level 4 and Level 5 programmes in Computing provide a university-level pathway, equivalent to the first two years of a UK Bachelor’s degree in a computing discipline. The Cyber Security pathway develops technical and professional skills for roles in cyber defence, security operations and related fields. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Typical tuition fee: €1,400 for Level 4 and €1,400 for Level 5.

ATHE Level 4 – Extended Diploma in Computing (Cyber Security Route)

Awarding Body: ATHE Level 4 Year 1 University-Level

Level 4 develops core computing knowledge and skills alongside specialist study in cyber security. Learners build a strong foundation in computer systems, networking and software, before focusing on security concepts and controls. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Example Core Units (Cyber Security Pathway)

  • Computer Systems, Networks and Security – hardware, operating systems, networking principles and risks to systems and data.
  • Programming and Software Development – fundamental programming constructs and software design techniques.
  • Database Design and Development – designing and implementing relational databases for realistic scenarios.
  • Web Technologies – client- and server-side concepts for delivering secure web applications.
  • Cyber Security Concepts – threats, vulnerabilities, risk assessment and incident response at an introductory level. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Programme Information

  • Level: 4 – approximately equivalent to the first year of a UK degree.
  • Mode: Classroom-based with lab work and guided independent study.
  • Assessment: Assignments, practical projects and case-study work.
  • Typical Tuition Fee: €1,400

ATHE Level 5 – Extended Diploma in Computing (Cyber Security Route)

Awarding Body: ATHE Level 5 Year 2 University-Level

Level 5 builds on the foundations from Level 4 and focuses on more advanced topics such as network security, cyber defence, penetration testing and secure systems development, preparing learners for direct entry to the final year of a degree or for specialist technical roles. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Example Core Units (Cyber Security Pathway)

  • Cyber Security – organisational security, industry standards and recommending improvements to secure networks and systems.
  • Network Security and Cryptography – securing network infrastructures and applying encryption principles.
  • Penetration Testing and Ethical Hacking – concepts, tools and methods used to test system defences in a controlled way. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
  • Secure Software Development – designing and building applications with security in mind from the outset.
  • Incident Response and Digital Forensics – responding to security incidents and gathering digital evidence appropriately.

Programme Information

  • Level: 5 – approximately equivalent to the second year of a UK degree.
  • Mode: Classroom teaching, labs and project-based learning.
  • Assessment: Larger projects, technical reports and scenario-based tasks.
  • Typical Tuition Fee: €1,400

Progression Route

  • Complete Level 4 (Computing – Cyber Security route).
  • Progress to Level 5 (advanced Cyber Security and Networking).
  • Progression to final-year BSc (Hons) with partner universities, subject to entry requirements.
  • Preparation for roles such as junior security analyst, network technician or support engineer.

Admissions Overview

  • Entry to Level 4: Normally Level 3 qualification in a related subject or equivalent experience.
  • Entry to Level 5: Completion of a suitable Level 4 Computing qualification.
  • Language: Teaching mainly in English; support available for local learners.
