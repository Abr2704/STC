Electronics

The Electronics pathway focuses on the principles and practical skills underpinning modern electronic systems. Learners gain experience with components, circuits and fault-finding, preparing them for further study or technical roles in industry.

Programmes are influenced by British vocational approaches and emphasise hands-on learning in laboratory and workshop settings.

Key Topics

  • Electronic components and circuit theory
  • Analogue and digital electronics
  • Printed circuit board (PCB) design concepts
  • Measurement, testing and fault-finding techniques
  • Introduction to microcontrollers and basic programming
  • Health and safety in the electronics laboratory

Laboratory and Practical Work

Practical sessions allow learners to build and test circuits, use electronic test equipment and develop confidence working with tools and components. This supports the development of both technical skills and safe working practices.

Progression and Pathways

Electronics study can support progression into:

  • Further or higher-level study in electronics, engineering or related subjects.
  • Technical support roles, workshop roles or junior technician positions.
  • Enhanced skills for those working in related technical fields.
